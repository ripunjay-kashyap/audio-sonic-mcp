# What I would do next

## 1. Add temporal stability weighting

This is probably the biggest gain.

Right now you likely aggregate:

```text id="v4h7xr"
global_chroma = average(all_frames)
```

Instead:

```text id="6q9d8r"
stable_sections get higher weight
transition/noise sections get lower weight
```

Why?
Because intros/outros/skits destroy rap key detection.

For Not Like Us:

* intro is ambiguous,
* groove section is more stable,
* hook reinforces tonic perception.

---

# 2. Detect “tonic persistence”

This is THE missing ingredient.

Humans hear key from:

* where tension resolves,
* where phrases land,
* where bass repeatedly returns.

Not just note frequency.

You need a feature like:

```text id="n83k5u"
tonic_persistence(note) =
how often musical phrases return to this note
```

This matters more than raw chroma energy.

---

# 3. Prioritize low-frequency harmonic content

For hip-hop:

## bass > chords

Seriously.

Try:

* extracting sub/808 harmonic fundamental,
* weighting low-end tonic recurrence heavily.

You’ll probably suddenly see:

* F,
* B,
* or modal tonic centers rise dramatically.

Most key detectors fail because they trust synth mids more than bass gravity.

---

# 5. Add ambiguity detection

This is where your system can surpass TuneBat.

If:

* top 2 scores are too close,
* AND no strong cadence exists,
* AND tonal stability is low,

then classify:

```json id="u3vlm8"
"mode": "ambiguous/modal"
```

instead of hallucinating certainty.

This is a professional MIR approach.

---

# 6. Add “groove-lock” detection

This is VERY important for rap.

The true tonic often becomes clear only after:

* drums,
* bass,
* and vocal cadence
  all align.

You can detect:

* rhythmic repetition stabilization,
* low-end recurrence,
* phrase periodicity.

Then:

## only estimate final key after groove stabilization.

This alone may fix many trap/rap tracks.

---

# 7. Use vocal melody as tonic evidence

Underrated trick.

Rappers repeatedly land on:

* tonic,
* fifth,
* minor third,
  during cadence endings.

Those are extremely valuable signals.

Humans unconsciously use vocals heavily for key perception.
